Poke Bowl

Description

Salmon poke bowls are easy to make! You can put whatever toppings you like. This recipe takes about 25 minutes to prep and offer four serving sizes!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cooked rice
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 4 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon chile oil (optional)
  • 3/4 pound sashimi-grade salmon, cut into small cubes

Toppings

  • 1 avacado, sliced, or to taste
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced or more to taste
  • 1/4 cup shelled edameme, or more to taste
  • 4 sheets fried seaweed, cut into strips, or to taste
  • 1 tabespoon pickled geinger, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon furikake (Japanese nori seasoning), or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onion, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon seasme seeds, or to taste

Steps

  1. Divide rice among 4 bowls.
  2. Mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and chile oil in a bowl to make dressing. Mix salmon with the dressing. Divide among the 4 bowls.
  3. Top bowls with avocado, red bell pepper, edamame, seaweed, pickled ginger, and furikake seasoning. Garnish with chopped green onion a sprinkling of sesame seeds.

Cook's Note

  • Substitute lettuce for the rice or use a mixture of both. Substitute sashimi-grade tuna for the salmon, or use tofu or mushrooms. Add finely chopped pollock if desired.
  • Use tamari or liquid aminos if the dressing needs to be gluten free.
  • Here are some other topping ideas: wakame (seaweed salad), fresh tobiko (fish eggs), thinly sliced omelet, diced pineapple and/or mango.
