Jellied Eels

Description

Jellied eels are an iconic dish with a rich history dating back to London, Engalnd, where they once were a street food sensation. This recipe offers a glimpse into this Old World tradition with a fresh twist. This gastronomic British masterpiece takes about 45-50 minutes to prepare.

Ingredients

  • Fresh eels
  • Parsley and pimentos (for flavoring)
  • Salt
  • Gelatine granules

Steps

  1. Start by cleaning the eels, ensuring that you remove the guts. Cut the eels into portions, making them manageable for cooking.
  2. To ensure safety, stun the eels so they are ready to be worked on. This step ensures that the eels won't move and make the process hazardous.
  3. In a large pot, add a bouquet garni of parsley and pimentos to flavor the water. This infusion of herbs imparts a unique taste and aroma to the dish.
  4. Bring the water to a boil, and add the eels. The cooking time will vary depending on the nature of the eel. Winter eels may take longer to cook (about 45-50 minutes) to achieve a softer flesh.
  5. During the cooking process, occasionally skim off any sediment and fat that rises to the surface of the water.
  6. Towards the end of the cooking, add salt for seasoning. This step ensures that your jellied eels are perfectly seasoned and balanced.
  7. Once the eels are thoroughly cooked, carefully remove them from the pot, allowing them to cool.
  8. Add gelatine granules to the cooking liquid, suspending it with salt to create the signature jellied texture.
  9. Place the eels in bowls and pour the jellied cooking liquid over them. Seal the bowls with lids and allow them to rest for about a day. During this time, the gelatine will set, and the eels will slowly float to the top, creating the desired consistency.
  10. To serve, take a piece of jellied eel from the bowl. You can enjoy it as it is or add a splash of non-brewed vinegar for an extra layer of flavor.

Cooking Tips

Jellied eels are traditionally served cold, but you can let them reach a slightly warmer room temperature for enhanced flavor.

The dish pairs wonderfully with a refreshing beverage, making it an ideal choice for snacking or a light meal.

Experiment with your preferred seasonings or condiments to personalize the flavor to your liking.
